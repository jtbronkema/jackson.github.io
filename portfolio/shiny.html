Problem Statement

The sports gambling industry has seen a rapid ascent in recent years. This is evident in the fact that there has been a large increase in the amount of money spent in sports gambling and the number of people who participate in sports betting both online and in casinos. With this knowledge me and three of my classmates decided to create an all inclusive sports betting information hob in our Using R for Analytics class. To create this application, we used the Shiny package through R. Some of the features that we have included in this application are current twitter feeds with up-to-date information on the NBA, recent team and player statistics, a player profile that compares players against each other, clustering analysis, and single game predictor tool with a probability of the winner.

Application Functionality

Our analytics dashboard consists of 5 pages. The first page includes hype video and gives a bief description of the NBA to those who do not know much about the NBA. The second page is the Team Profile. The team profile page gives team statistics, player statistics, and salary information for each NBA team. The Players Profile page allows users to compare the worht of different NBA players in a one-vs-one manner using deep statistics. There is also page to look at many different statistics for each player without comparing them to another player. In the fourth page of the application, we have cluster analysis. This aspect of the application allows users to cluster all current NBA players based on their total worth to a team. This is a great application for NBA executives or Fantasy Basketball players to use in order to pick the NBA players to their team truly have the most value. The most importatnt aspect of our application is in the final page. This is a game predictiontool. For any possible match-up of NBA teams, or tool uses logistic regression in order to predict which team will win and it also gives the probability that that team will win.
